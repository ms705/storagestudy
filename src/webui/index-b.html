I am a PhD student in the NetOS division of the Systems Research Group at the University of Cambridge Computer Laboratory, under the supervision of Dr Steven Hand.

On this page, you can find information about my research and teaching activities. I also have a personal website.

Research

In am interested in the general area of ubiquitous storage systems for personal data. In particular, I am interested in systems supporting variable trade-offs for diverse storage requirements, both on the infrastructure side and in ways of developing ubiquitous access.
My undergraduate dissertation was in the area of Computer Vision, focussing on interactive 3D structure-from-motion. More information about it can be found on the Proteus project page.
